[Miami](/cities/miami) is a city on the southeast coast of Florida, a warm, Latin-inflected metropolis of beaches, Art Deco architecture, and a deep Cuban and Latin American food culture. Most first trips fix on South Beach, but the city beyond it is the better half. A first visit is three or four days. This guide covers getting in, where to stay, the neighborhoods and beaches, and where to eat.

## How many days do I need?

Three or four days is the shape of a Miami trip. That covers the beaches and the Art Deco District, the Wynwood murals, and Little Havana, with time to eat across the city's Latin American range.

Miami is also the launch point for the Florida Keys and the Everglades. If either is on the list, add a few days and a rental car.

## How do I get in from the airport?

**Miami International Airport (MIA)** sits about 8 miles from the beaches. Rideshare and taxis are the usual way in, around 20 to 30 minutes to South Beach depending on traffic. The Metrorail connects the airport to downtown.

Miami is a spread-out, car-oriented city. **Fort Lauderdale airport** to the north is the other arrival option, often cheaper for flights. Once here, plan on rideshare or a rental car rather than relying on transit.

## Where should I stay?

Pick a base by the kind of trip you want.

| Area | Why pick it | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| **South Beach** | On the sand, in the Art Deco and nightlife scene, the classic first-trip base | Busy, pricey, and loud at night |
| **Brickell** | The modern downtown high-rise district, walkable, strong for dining | A city base rather than a beach one |
| **Wynwood / Design District** | The arts-led base, murals, galleries, and bars | Not on the beach, quieter after dark |
| **Mid-Beach** | Still on the ocean but calmer than South Beach | Further from the nightlife and the Art Deco strip |

## The neighborhoods and beaches

**South Beach** is the postcard: the wide sandy beach, the lifeguard towers, and the **Art Deco District** along Ocean Drive and Collins Avenue, a dense run of pastel 1930s buildings best seen on foot in the morning light.

**Wynwood** is the former warehouse district turned arts quarter, anchored by the **Wynwood Walls** outdoor murals and now full of breweries, galleries, and restaurants. **Little Havana**, centered on Calle Ocho (SW 8th Street), is the heart of Cuban Miami, with cigar shops, the domino park, and the cafecito counters. **Brickell** is the glassy downtown financial district, and downtown holds [Bayside Marketplace](/pins/bayside-marketplace), the waterfront shopping and dining complex on Biscayne Bay, a departure point for bay cruises.

The beaches run the length of the barrier island, from South Beach north through Mid-Beach and beyond, all wide, warm, and free.

## Where to eat

Miami eats on Cuban and Latin American cooking above all, from Cuban sandwiches and cafecito to Argentine grills and Peruvian kitchens, with a high-end scene on top.
