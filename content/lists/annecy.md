[Annecy](/cities/annecy) is a town of about 130,000 people in the French Alps, set at the northern tip of Lake Annecy, one of the cleanest lakes in Europe. The old town is threaded by branches of the Thiou canal and overlooked by a hilltop castle, the lake is clean enough to swim in, and the mountains rise straight from the water. A day or two covers it. This guide covers getting in, where to stay, the old town and the lake, the markets, and where to eat.

## How long do I need?

One full day covers the old town and the lakefront. A second day adds a longer walk or cycle around the lake, a summer swim, or a boat trip out onto the water.

Annecy is a town to slow down in rather than rush. It pairs easily with Geneva, 45 minutes north, or works as a stop on a wider French Alps trip toward Chamonix.

## How do I get to Annecy?

The closest airport is **Geneva**, about 45 minutes away by road or direct bus, with **Lyon** about an hour and a half. By rail, **TGV trains from Paris** reach Annecy in roughly four hours.

Annecy station is a short walk from both the old town and the lake, so you do not need a car once you arrive. The town itself is small and best seen on foot.

## The old town and the lake

The **old town**, the Vieille Ville, is the heart of Annecy, a tight grid of pastel arcaded houses split by branches of the Thiou canal, which has earned the town the overused nickname "Venice of the Alps." Sitting mid-canal is the [Palais de l'Île](/pins/le-palais-de-i-ile), a sharp-prowed 12th-century building that has been a courthouse and a prison and is now a small museum, and the most photographed object in town.

Above the old town, **Annecy Castle**, the former home of the Counts of Geneva, looks down from its hill. At the lake end of the old town, the **Pont des Amours** crosses the canal toward the [Jardins de l'Europe](/pins/jardins-de-l-europe), the lakeside park of mature trees that opens onto the water.

**Lake Annecy** itself is the other half of the visit. A flat path circles much of the shore for walking and cycling, the water is swimmable through the warm months, and pedalos and small boats are easy to rent from the waterfront.

## The markets

Annecy runs one of the better markets in the region. The main **old-town market** fills the canal-side streets on **Tuesday, Friday, and Sunday mornings** with produce, mountain cheeses, charcuterie, and Savoyard specialties. The [Annecy farmers market](/pins/annecy-farmers-market) is the place to taste your way through the local Alpine food. A separate [market on the boulevard Taine](/pins/marche-du-boulevard-taine) adds another option. If a market matters to the trip, plan around one of those mornings.

## Where to eat

Annecy eats Savoyard: Alpine cheese in every form, from raclette and fondue to tartiflette, alongside lake fish and the produce off the market stalls.
