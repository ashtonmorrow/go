[Chur](/cities/chur) is the oldest continuously inhabited town in Switzerland and the practical launch point for two of the country's scenic-train routes. The old town is small, the Bernina Express to Tirano leaves from the station, and the Glacier Express to Zermatt connects through the same platforms. Most travelers see Chur as a one-night stop. Here's how to use it.

## Getting in from Zurich

Chur sits about 120 km southeast of Zurich. SBB intercity trains run direct from Zurich HB to Chur every 30 minutes, the journey takes about 75 minutes.

| Mode | Time | Cost | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| SBB intercity train | 75 min | ~CHF 38 second class, ~CHF 60 first class | The default. Direct from Zurich HB, every 30 minutes. The Swiss Travel Pass covers the route |
| Car via A3/A13 | ~100 min | Rental + ~CHF 35 vignette + fuel | Useful only if you are continuing into the Engadin or the Italian lakes. Otherwise the train is faster door-to-door |
| Bus PostBus from Zurich | 2 hr 30 min | ~CHF 25 | Cheaper than the train, slower and less reliable |

From Zurich Airport (ZRH) the same SBB trains run direct to Chur in about 85 minutes, no transfer through Zurich HB needed.

## Why come to Chur

Three reasons. The Bernina Express leaves from the station, with the route into Tirano (Italy) one of the most-photographed Alpine rail rides in the world. The Glacier Express runs the other direction toward Zermatt. And the old town itself is small, walkable, and a thousand years older than most of central Europe.

If you are not riding either scenic train, two days is too much. One night on the way to or from Tirano fits most trips.

## Where to stay

The old town is compact enough that any central hotel puts everything within walking distance. The atlas does not yet carry hotel pins for Chur, so book on the usual sites and read recent reviews for the property.

A few neighborhood notes for picking by feel rather than chain:

- **Postplatz / Bahnhof.** The cluster near the train station is the easiest base when you are catching the Bernina Express the next morning. Roll the bag five minutes to the platform.
- **Old town.** The medieval lanes around Arcas Square and the Cathedral are the more atmospheric base. A few small boutique hotels and B&Bs sit on the slope.
- **Arosa or Lenzerheide.** Both are an hour up the valley by PostBus or the Arosabahn. Useful as a longer base if hiking or skiing is the reason for the trip, not for a one-night Bernina stop.

## What to see

For a one-night Chur stop, two walks cover the practical agenda.

The **old town walking circuit** runs about 90 minutes if you stop for the cathedral and the museum. Start at Postplatz, walk up Reichsgasse to the Obertor (the medieval upper gate), then loop back through Arcas Square (the painted-facade plaza locals use as the postcard) and the cathedral close. The **Cathedral of St Mary** is the 12th-century anchor, free to enter. The **Rätisches Museum** at the same square is the regional history museum, around CHF 8, useful if Roman-era Graubünden interests you.

The **Haldenstein side trip** is the other half-day worth doing if you have time. [Haldenstein Castle](/pins/haldenstein-castle) sits across the Rhine from Chur, about 4 km out, and is a working castle ruin with the 16th-century courtyard largely intact. PostBus or a 50-minute walk along the river. Free to wander.

## The scenic train connections

This is the actual reason most travelers route through Chur.

The **[Bernina Express](/lists/bernina-express)** runs from Chur to Tirano (Italy) in about 4 hours, crossing the Bernina Pass at 2,253 m. The route is a UNESCO World Heritage rail line. Panoramic-windowed cars, reservation required, around CHF 66 second class plus the reservation fee. Book ahead in summer.

The **Glacier Express** runs Chur to Zermatt in about 8 hours, billed as the "slowest express train in the world." Same panoramic-window format, also reservation required, around CHF 145 second class plus reservation. The route crosses the Oberalp Pass.

If you are doing both, plan two nights in Chur: one on either side of the chosen route, or one each direction if you have time. The trains do not run twice a day, so reverse legs need to fit the schedule.
