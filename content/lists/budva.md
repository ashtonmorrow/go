[Budva](/cities/budva) is a small walled Old Town wrapped in a loud beach resort, the busiest stretch of the Montenegrin coast. Two or three days covers it, and its real value is as a base for the rest of the coast, Kotor and Sveti Stefan included. Here's how I'd play it.

## How long do I need?

Two or three days is the shape of a Budva trip. The walled Old Town can be seen in a few hours, and the rest is beach time, an evening out, and short trips along the coast.

Budva is a summer town. It is loudest and fullest in July and August, and late spring and early autumn are the easier windows. Used as a base for the wider riviera, it could hold a longer stay.

## How do I get to Budva?

Budva has no airport of its own. **Tivat Airport** is the closest, about 20 km away, with **Podgorica Airport** around 65 km and **Dubrovnik** in Croatia about 70 km, the last involving a border crossing.

Buses run frequently along the coast road, linking Budva with Kotor, Tivat, and the other riviera towns. A car is useful for reaching the coastal day trips at your own pace.

## Where should I stay?

For a first visit, base in or near the **Old Town**. From there the historic quarter, the main beaches, and the restaurants are all on foot, and you are at the center of the evening scene.

The resort areas spreading north and south along the coast trade the central location for newer rooms and quieter beaches. The atlas does not yet carry hotel pins for Budva, so book on the usual sites and read recent reviews for the property.

## The Old Town and the beaches

The **Old Town**, Stari Grad, is the reason Budva is more than a beach strip, a compact Venetian-walled quarter of narrow stone lanes, small churches, and a seafront **citadel** built into the walls. It takes a couple of hours to walk and is best in the early evening.

The beaches are the rest of the trip. The town beach and **Mogren Beach**, reached by a cliff path from the Old Town, are the closest. Above Mogren, [Fort Mogren](/pins/fort-mogren) is a ruined Austro-Hungarian coastal fort, and the [Door in Stone](/pins/door-in-stone) is a rock feature along the same shoreline. The [Green Lagoon](/pins/green-lagoon) is one of the swimming spots around the bay.

## Day trips along the coast

Budva's position makes it a base for the Montenegrin coast. **Sveti Stefan**, the much-photographed fortified islet linked to the shore by a causeway, is a short trip south. It operates as a private resort, so the classic view is from the mainland. **Kotor**, with its dramatic walled old town under the mountains at the head of a deep bay, is under an hour north and one of the finest stops on the Adriatic. The whole riviera is connected by the coastal bus.

## Where to eat

Budva eats on Adriatic cooking: grilled fish and seafood, Montenegrin meat and cheese from the interior, and the resort-town range of international options.
