[Athens](/cities/athens) is the city most travellers underestimate going in and oversell coming out. Two days is enough to do the headline sights well; three is the sweet spot once you add a day trip; four if you want Delphi as an overnight. The metro and walking handle everything central. Pickpockets and the midday queue at the Acropolis are the two friction points to plan around. The full pin map sits below; this writeup covers the parts of it that change the trip.

## Getting in

Athens International Airport (ATH) is 33 km from central Athens. The metro M3 runs straight from the airport to Syntagma Square in 40 minutes for €9 single (€16 return). Express buses run 24 hours for €6. Taxis are flat-rate €40 daytime, €55 nighttime to central Athens; make sure the meter starts at zero and confirm the flat rate before pulling away.

## Where to stay

Three useful bases, all walking-distance from the Acropolis.

| Where | Hotel | Why pick it | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monastiraki / Psyrri | Various boutiques with rooftop Acropolis views | The lively core. Bars, tavernas, the flea market | Loud weekend evenings |
| Plaka | Plaka boutiques | Slightly slower than Monastiraki, same proximity to the Acropolis | Very tourist-priced |
| Makrygianni (south of Acropolis) | [Grand Hyatt Athens](/pins/grand-hyatt-athens-4e8c) | Upscale, the best rooftop pool in central Athens, walking distance to the Acropolis via the south entrance | A 15-minute walk from Monastiraki proper |

Most mid-tier hotels here have a rooftop with an Acropolis view. Check the photos before booking; the rooftop view is often the deciding factor.

## The Acropolis pass

The single best ticket purchase in Athens. The combination pass costs €30 (€15 low season, Nov to Mar) and includes:

| Site | Why visit |
|---|---|
| [Acropolis of Athens](/pins/acropolis-of-athens) and [Parthenon](/pins/parthenon) | The headline. 8 a.m. or 5 p.m. is the move |
| [The Athenian Agora](/pins/the-athenian-agora-athens-greece) | The civic centre of classical Athens. The reconstructed Stoa of Attalos is a museum |
| [Roman Forum of Athens (Roman Agora)](/pins/roman-forum-of-athens-roman-agora) and [Tower of the Winds](/pins/tower-of-the-winds) | A 5-minute walk from the Athenian Agora. The Tower of the Winds is the oldest weather station in the world |
| [Theatre of Dionysus](/pins/theatre-of-dionysus) | The birthplace of European theatre. On the south slope of the Acropolis |
| Hadrian's Library | Off Monastiraki Square. 15-minute visit |
| Kerameikos | The ancient cemetery. Underrated, usually quiet |
| Temple of Olympian Zeus | The 15 surviving columns of the largest temple in Greece. 20-minute visit, walking distance from the Acropolis |

The pass is valid 5 days. Buy online to avoid the ticket-window queue.

Separately ticketed: the [Acropolis Museum](/pins/acropolis-museum) (€20, separate building south of the Acropolis, holds the actual sculptures) and the [National Archaeological Museum](/pins/national-archeological-museum) (€12, north of Omonia, the country's most comprehensive collection). Both are worth the price.

## Plaka and Anafiotika

The evening walk worth taking. [Anafiotika](/pins/anafiotika) is the tiny Cycladic-style village built into the north slope of the Acropolis, easy to miss if you do not look up. [Plaka](/cities/athens) is the surrounding neighbourhood: narrow lanes, tavernas with outdoor tables, the [Monastiraki Flea Market](/pins/monastiraki-flea-market) at the bottom of the hill, and the surprising [Monastiraki metro archaeology site](/pins/monastiraki-metro-station-archaelogical-site-greece) which you can walk past for free inside the station.

For sunset views without the climb: [Philopappos Monument](/pins/philopappos-monument) hill is a 20-minute walk up with a clear Parthenon view, no ticket, no queue. [Daphne Viewpoint](/pins/daphne-viewpoint) is a quieter alternative. The [Lycabettus Funicular](/pins/lycabettus-funicular) takes you to the city's other peak with the Acropolis on the skyline and 360-degree views.

## Day trips

| Trip | Time | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cape Sounion (Temple of Poseidon) | Half-day, 2 hrs each way | €6.90 round-trip on KTEL Bus X80, or €120 to €150 private taxi-tour | Coastal drive south. Sunset slot is the photograph everyone takes |
| Daphni Monastery | Half-day | €5 entry, metro M3 to Agia Marina + bus | UNESCO Byzantine mosaics. Combined-site entry includes [Daphni, Hosios Loukas, and Nea Moni of Chios](/pins/monasteries-of-daphni-hosios-loukas-and-nea-moni-of-chios) |
| Aegina | Day trip | €15 round-trip ferry from Piraeus | Saronic island, pistachio production, the Temple of Aphaia. Take the morning Hellenic Seaways fast ferry |
| Delphi | Overnight | €15 KTEL bus each way, ~3 hrs | Pythia's oracle, museum, archaeological site. Stay one night to do it properly; Athens day trip leaves you hurrying |
