[Istanbul](/cities/istanbul) is more city than one trip can hold, 15 million people across two continents, with Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman history stacked under a fast modern metropolis. Four or five days, and you still won't see all of it. Here's how I'd choose.

## How many days do I need?

Four or five days covers a first visit: the old city monuments, the bazaars, a Bosphorus trip, and the modern districts, with a crossing to the Asian side.

Istanbul is vast and layered, and the planning move is to choose rather than chase. Even a week leaves things for a return trip, so build the days around a short list and leave room to wander.

## How do I get in from the airport?

Istanbul has **two airports**. **Istanbul Airport (IST)** on the European side is the main international hub, connected by metro and airport buses, roughly an hour to the center. **Sabiha Gökçen (SAW)** is on the Asian side, used by many budget carriers, and is further out.

From either, the metro and bus network or a booked transfer will get you in. Both airports sit well outside the center, so allow time. In the city itself, the trams, metro, funiculars, and ferries cover most of what you need on a rechargeable Istanbulkart.

## Where should I stay?

The choice is between the old city and the modern center.

**Sultanahmet**, the old city, puts Hagia Sophia, the Blue Mosque, and the bazaars within walking distance, the convenient base for first-time sightseeing. **Beyoğlu**, across the Golden Horn around İstiklal Street and Galata, is the livelier modern base, stronger for restaurants, bars, and nightlife. Many visitors stay in Beyoğlu and take the short tram or walk to the old city.

For a hotel on the Beyoğlu side, [InterContinental Istanbul](/pins/intercontinental-istanbul) is the lodging pin on the list. The property is plush, the rooms read as "old luxury" rather than modern, and the views from a high floor are the entire pitch. Beyond it the atlas does not yet carry a wide hotel pin set for Istanbul, so book on the usual sites and read recent reviews for the property.

## The old city and the Bosphorus

The **old city**, Sultanahmet, holds the major monuments. **Hagia Sophia**, the great domed church turned mosque turned museum and now a working mosque again, and the **Blue Mosque** face each other across a park. **Topkapı Palace**, the Ottoman sultans' palace, and the sunken **Basilica Cistern** are both close by, and both sell timed tickets worth booking ahead.

The bazaars are the other half of the old city: the vast covered **Grand Bazaar** and the smaller, fragrant **Spice Bazaar** near the water. Across the Golden Horn, **Beyoğlu** rises around the medieval **Galata Tower** and the long pedestrian İstiklal Street.

The single thing not to skip is the water. A **Bosphorus ferry**, and a crossing to the Asian side at **Kadıköy** for the food and the markets, are what make the city's geography click into place.

## Where to eat

Istanbul eats on a deep Turkish range: kebabs and grilled meats, mezze, fresh fish by the water, the simit and börek of the street, and a strong cafe culture. Both the old city and Beyoğlu are dense with options.
