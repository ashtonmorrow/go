Krka National Park is the Croatian river park built around a chain of travertine waterfalls, with Skradinski Buk, the wide terraced falls, as the centerpiece. It is an easy day trip from Split or the cathedral city of Šibenik. Go before the tour boats arrive. Here's how I'd time it.

## How long do I need?

Half a day covers the main sight. [Skradinski Buk](/pins/skradinski-buk-waterfall), the terraced waterfall the park is known for, and the boardwalks that loop around it take two to three hours at a relaxed pace.

Adding a national-park boat trip upriver, to the Roški Slap falls or the small island monastery of Visovac, turns Krka into a full day. Most visitors do the park as a day trip and sleep in Šibenik, Skradin, or Split.

## How do I get to Krka National Park?

Krka sits in Dalmatia, close to the coastal city of Šibenik. The nearest airports are **Split**, about an hour southeast, and **Zadar**, about an hour northwest.

Buses run from Split and Zadar to Šibenik and on to Skradin, but the park entrances are easiest to reach by car. Many visitors drive, and many more join an organized day tour from Split or the Dalmatian resort towns. The [tributary of the Čikola](/pins/tributary-cikola-into-krka) into the Krka, the river canyon on the approach, is part of the same protected landscape.

## Skradin or Lozovac?

Krka has two main entrances, and the choice changes how you arrive.

| Entrance | How you reach the falls | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Skradin** | A national-park boat runs up the river from the [Skradinski buk ferry pier](/pins/skradinski-buk-ferry-pier) to the falls. Scenic, and mostly a warm-season service | Visitors staying in Skradin, or anyone wanting the river approach |
| **[Lozovac](/pins/krka-waterfalls-entrance-at-lozovac)** | Park at the entrance, ride a free shuttle bus down to Skradinski Buk. Reliable and open year-round | Drivers, off-season visits, and the simplest logistics |

Buy tickets online in advance for any summer visit. Park entry prices are seasonal and highest in July and August.

## Where should I stay?

Two bases make sense, for two kinds of trip.

**Šibenik** is the larger base, a working coastal city with a UNESCO cathedral, an old town of stone lanes, and a full range of restaurants and hotels. **Skradin** is the small, pretty riverside town right at the park's northern gate, calmer and closer to the water. For a hotel with a marina setting near Šibenik, [D-Resort Šibenik](/pins/d-resort-sibenik) sits on the waterfront by the city's marina.

## The waterfalls

[Skradinski Buk](/pins/skradinski-buk-waterfall) is the reason most people come, a 400-meter run of terraced travertine cascades dropping into a wide pool, circled by boardwalks and footbridges. It is photogenic and, in high summer, busy. An early entry, before the tour groups arrive in late morning, is the difference between a quiet walk and a slow shuffle.

One change to plan around: **swimming at Skradinski Buk has been banned since 2021**, to protect the fragile travertine that builds the falls. The waterfalls are for looking now. For a swim, the river beaches around Skradin are the answer, including [City Beach Skradin](/pins/city-beach-skradin) and [Trutin beach](/pins/trutin-beach) on the calm water near the town.

## The gateway city

Šibenik is the coastal city beside the park and the better base for anyone wanting a town as well as a waterfall. Unlike most Dalmatian cities, it was founded by Croatians rather than Greeks or Romans, and it climbs the hillside in a tangle of stone streets and stairways.

The landmark is the **Cathedral of St James**, a 15th and 16th-century church built entirely of stone, with no brick or timber, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Above the old town, **St. Michael's Fortress** gives the wide view and hosts open-air concerts in summer. Out at the mouth of the St. Anthony Channel, [St. Nicholas' Fortress](/pins/st-nicholas-fortress) is the 16th-century Venetian sea fort guarding the harbor entrance, itself UNESCO-listed as part of the Venetian defensive works.

## Where to eat and drink

The eating around Krka is Dalmatian: grilled fish, lamb, olive oil, and the wines of the Šibenik hinterland. [Rak Winery](/pins/rak-winery) is a local producer worth a tasting stop on a drive through the area.

Out on the islands off Šibenik, [Bar Fingac](/pins/bar-fingac) is a bar on car-free Zlarin, and [Obrt Antonia Beach Bar Vila](/pins/obrt-antonia-beach-bar-vila) is a beach-bar stop for a drink by the water. Both are warm-season places, reached by the local ferries.
