[Mostar](/cities/mostar) is a small Herzegovina town on the green Neretva, built around the Stari Most, the Ottoman bridge shelled to rubble in the 1990s and rebuilt in 2004. The old core is tiny, but the buses pour in midday, so an overnight is the better trip. Here's how I'd time it.

## How long do I need?

One day covers the old core, the Old Bridge, the bazaar, and a mosque or two. But Mostar fills with day-trip buses from the coast through the middle of the day, and the old town gets shoulder-to-shoulder.

An overnight is the better trip. It gives you the bridge in early-morning and late-evening light, the bazaar quiet, and a calm dinner after the buses have gone. A second day adds [Kravica Waterfall](/pins/kravica-waterfall) and the village of Blagaj.

## How do I get to Mostar?

Mostar sits between Sarajevo and the Adriatic coast. The **scenic train from Sarajevo** takes about two and a half hours, threading the Neretva canyon, and it is a journey worth doing for itself.

**Buses** connect Mostar with Sarajevo, Dubrovnik, and Split. Many visitors arrive on a day trip by bus or tour from Dubrovnik or Split, which is exactly the midday crowd an overnight lets you sidestep.

## Where should I stay?

Stay in or near the **Old Town**, the cluster of cobbled lanes around the bridge. From there the Stari Most, the mosques, and the restaurants are all on foot, and you wake up to the town before the day-trippers.

## The Old Bridge and the Old Town

The **Stari Most** is the reason most people come, a single stone arch leaping the Neretva, first built by the Ottomans in the 16th century, shelled to rubble in 1993, and rebuilt with original techniques and reopened in 2004. It is both a beautiful object and a plain lesson in what the war cost. The [Old Bridge photo point](/pins/mostar-old-bridge-photopoint) is the spot for the classic view of the arch over the river.

Members of the local divers' club still leap from the bridge into the cold river through the warmer months, usually after working the crowd for tips. It is their tradition, not a visitor activity.

Around the bridge runs the **Kujundžiluk**, the old bazaar, a cobbled lane of copper workshops, rug stalls, and cafes. The **Koski Mehmed-Pasha Mosque** is worth the small fee to climb its minaret for the bridge view. For sunset, the [Mostar city viewpoint](/pins/mostar-city-viewpoint-for-sunset) looks over the town and the river, and on a hot day the [Gradski bazen](/pins/gradski-bazen) is the town pool.

## Kravica Waterfall and the day trips

[Kravica Waterfall](/pins/kravica-waterfall) is the standout trip from Mostar, a wide curtain of falls on the Trebižat River, about 40 minutes south, with pools you can swim in below the cascades in summer. It is busiest midday, so go early.

The other easy trip is **Blagaj**, a village about 15 minutes from Mostar where the Blagaj Tekke, a white Ottoman dervish house, stands against a cliff at the source of the Buna River. Počitelj, a stepped Ottoman hill village, sits along the same road south.

## Where to eat

Mostar eats Bosnian: grilled ćevapi, slow-cooked meat and vegetable dishes, burek, and strong coffee served in the Ottoman style. The bazaar restaurants over the river are scenic, and the ones a street back are usually better value.
