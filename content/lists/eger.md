[Eger](/cities/eger) is the baroque wine town two hours from Budapest, famous in Hungary for a 1552 siege its castle somehow won and famous to everyone else for the red wine poured in the cellars on its edge. A day does the castle and old town. The wine valley is why you stay the night.

## Getting in from Budapest

**Direct trains** run from Budapest to Eger in about two hours through the day. Eger's station sits a 15-to-20-minute walk from the old town, or a short bus ride. Inside Eger everything in this guide is walkable except the wine valley, which is a 20-minute walk or a cheap taxi from the center.

Eger is a long day trip rather than a quick one. Two hours each way means the practical day is the castle, the basilica, and the baroque streets, with the train back before evening. To add the wine valley properly, stay over.

## The castle and the old town

**Eger Castle** crowns the hill above the town and is the anchor of any visit. Its fame rests on the 1552 siege, when the defenders held against an Ottoman force many times their size, an episode that became a fixture of Hungarian national storytelling. The hilltop site holds a history museum, the underground casemates, and the best view over the baroque rooftops below.

The **old town** is a compact baroque ensemble. Dobo Square, named for the captain who led the siege defense, is the center of it. The **Eger Basilica** is the large neoclassical cathedral, the second-largest church in Hungary. The **minaret** is the unexpected note, a 40-meter Ottoman tower, the northernmost surviving Ottoman minaret in Europe, a remnant of the decades the town spent under Ottoman rule. A tight internal staircase climbs it for anyone untroubled by narrow spaces.

For two smaller, more idiosyncratic stops, the [Beatles Museum](/pins/beatles-museum-road-eger-eger-hungary) is a private collection that is exactly the curiosity it sounds like, and the [Márai visitor center](/pins/marai-aktiv-turisztikai-latogatokozpont) is the active-tourism point for anyone planning to get out into the Bukk hills around the town.

## The Valley of the Beautiful Women

The **Szepasszony-volgy**, the Valley of the Beautiful Women, is the reason Eger rewards an overnight. A short walk or taxi from the center, it is a horseshoe of cellars cut straight into the hillside, each run by a winemaking family, pouring the local reds cheaply from the barrel. The wine to order is **Egri Bikaver**, the dry red blend known in English as Bull's Blood. The cellars are informal, the pours are inexpensive, and the evening is the experience. Go after dark, take it slowly, and do not plan to catch a late train.

## Where to stay and eat

Most visitors come for the day, but the wine valley makes the overnight worthwhile. [Imola Mansion Hotel](/pins/imola-mansion-hotel) is a comfortable base near the castle, an easy walk to both the old town and the start of the climb. The wider hotel range in Eger is small but reasonable, concentrated around the old town.

For a meal, [Macok Bisztró és Borbár](/pins/macok-bisztro-es-borbar) is the standout, a modern bistro and wine bar at the foot of the castle hill with a serious local-wine list, the right place to taste the region's reds without committing to the full cellar crawl.

## Festivals and big annual events

Eger's calendar leans, unsurprisingly, on wine.

| Event | When | What it changes |
|---|---|---|
| **Eger Wine Festival (Egri Bikavér Ünnep)** | July, several days | The town's headline event, a celebration of Bikavér and the regional wines around Dobó Square and the castle. Hotels fill, and the cellars are at their liveliest |
| **Harvest days (Szüreti napok)** | September | The autumn harvest festival, with a parade, folk music, and the new wine. The other strong window for a wine-focused visit |
| **Castle history days** | Summer, varying dates | Re-enactments and historical programming staged at the castle, leaning on the 1552 siege story. Family-scale |
| **Christmas market** | Advent, December | A compact market on Dobó Square. Smaller than the Budapest markets, a reasonable add-on rather than a reason to travel |

The trip-shaping windows are the July wine festival and the September harvest days, both of which put the region's wine front and center. Outside those, Eger is an easy year-round day trip from Budapest.
