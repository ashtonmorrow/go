[Izmir](/cities/izmir) is the Turkish city Istanbul makes everyone skip, and that is its charm: an easygoing Aegean port built for walking the waterfront, with Ephesus an hour down the road. Two or three unhurried days. Here's how I'd spend them.

## How many days do I need?

Two or three days is the shape of a first visit. The waterfront, Konak Square, and the old bazaar fill a day or two of unhurried walking, and a third day goes to Ephesus.

Izmir is a city to slow down in rather than rush. It does not have Istanbul's stack of monuments, and that is the point. With more time, the Aegean seaside towns of Çeşme and Alaçatı are an easy add for a longer coast trip.

## How do I get into Izmir?

**Izmir Adnan Menderes Airport (ADB)** sits south of the city. The **İZBAN** suburban rail line runs directly from the airport terminal into the city center, and it is the cheap, reliable way in. Taxis and rideshare also serve the airport and take roughly 30 to 45 minutes to the central districts depending on traffic.

Inside the city, the metro, the İZBAN trains, and the bay ferries cover the longer distances, and the central districts are walkable.

## Where should I stay?

Two central districts make sense for a first visit.

**Alsancak** is the lively district near the water, full of restaurants, bars, and cafes, and the easiest base for evenings out. **Konak** is the central core around Konak Square, well connected by metro and ferry and close to the bazaar. Both put the city on foot and a short ride from everything else. The atlas does not yet carry hotel pins for Izmir, so book on the usual sites and read recent reviews for the property.

## What to see in Izmir

Izmir is light on must-see monuments and strong on a few good days of walking.

The **Kordon** is the wide seafront promenade along the bay, the place the city walks, runs, and sits at sunset, and the right place to start. **Konak Square** is the central plaza, marked by the ornate 1901 **Clock Tower** that has become the city's symbol. Behind it spreads **Kemeraltı**, the historic covered bazaar, a working market of lanes, mosques, and tea houses good for a long wander.

The **Agora of Smyrna** is the excavated Roman marketplace in the middle of the modern city. For a view, the **Asansör**, a historic elevator built into a cliff in the Karataş district, lifts you to a terrace over the bay, and **Kadifekale**, the hilltop castle, gives the wider panorama.

## Is Ephesus a day trip from Izmir?

Yes, and it is the standout reason to base on this coast. **Ephesus**, one of the great ancient cities of the Mediterranean, sits near the town of Selçuk about an hour south of Izmir. The Library of Celsus, the Great Theatre, and the marble streets are remarkably intact.

Trains and buses run from Izmir to Selçuk, and organized day tours are easy to join. Go early in the day to get ahead of both the Aegean heat and the midday tour crowds. The site is large and largely unshaded, so bring water and a hat.

## Where should I eat?

Izmir eats on the Aegean: seafood, olive oil vegetable dishes, mezze, and the city's own street snacks like *boyoz* pastry and *kumru* sandwiches. The bazaar and Alsancak are the densest eating grounds.
