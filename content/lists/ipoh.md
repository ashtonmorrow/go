[Ipoh](/cities/ipoh) is the capital of Perak state in Malaysia, a city that grew rich on tin mining and sits in a valley ringed by dramatic limestone hills. It is known above all for its food, alongside a walkable colonial old town and a set of temples cut into the surrounding cliffs. A day or two covers it, and it makes an easy stop between Kuala Lumpur and Penang. This guide covers getting in, where to stay, the old town and the cave temples, and where to eat.

## How long do I need?

A day or two covers Ipoh. One day handles the colonial old town, a cave temple, and several meals. A second day adds more temples, the street art, and a slower pace.

Ipoh is most often visited as a stop between Kuala Lumpur and Penang, and it fits that role well. It is a food-and-wander town rather than a place with a long sightseeing list.

## How do I get to Ipoh?

Ipoh is on Malaysia's main west-coast rail line. Fast **ETS electric trains** run from **Kuala Lumpur** in around two to two and a half hours, and continue north toward Penang.

Ipoh has a small airport with limited routes, so most visitors arrive by train or bus. The restored colonial **railway station**, a white Moorish-style building locals nickname the Taj Mahal of Ipoh, is a landmark in its own right.

## Where should I stay?

Stay in or near the **old town**, which puts the colonial streets, the cafes, and the kopitiam coffee shops on foot. The city splits into the Old Town and the New Town across the Kinta River, both central.

## The old town and the cave temples

The **old town** is a walkable grid of British colonial shophouses and civic buildings, with the white **railway station** as its showpiece. Narrow **Concubine Lane** is the busy, touristy heritage alley at its heart, and a trail of **street-art murals** threads the surrounding streets.

The other half of Ipoh is the **cave temples**, Chinese temples built into the limestone hills around the city. **Sam Poh Tong**, **Perak Tong**, and **Kek Lok Tong** are the best known, each combining shrines and statuary with natural caverns, and some with gardens or viewpoints. They sit outside the center, so reach them by a short taxi or rideshare ride.

## Where to eat

This is the reason to come. Ipoh is one of Malaysia's most respected food towns, and the signatures are **Ipoh white coffee**, the smooth local brew, then **bean sprout chicken**, poached chicken with the crisp local sprouts, and a deep dim sum and kopitiam coffee-shop culture. Eat by following the busy old-school shops.
