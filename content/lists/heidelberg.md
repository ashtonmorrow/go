[Heidelberg](/cities/heidelberg) is the castle town that actually lives up to the pictures, a ruined red-sandstone castle on the hill above an intact medieval old town on the Neckar. It is small enough to see in a day and a short train from Frankfurt. Here's how I'd spend it.

## How long do I need?

One full day covers the main sights. That is enough for the castle, the [Heidelberg Altstadt](/pins/heidelberg-altstadt) and the Old Bridge, and the Philosophers' Walk across the river.

An overnight is the better version. It gets you the Old Town before the day-trip coaches arrive, a calmer evening on the Hauptstrasse, and the time to ride higher to the Königstuhl summit. Heidelberg also pairs easily with Frankfurt for a wider trip.

## How do I get to Heidelberg?

Heidelberg has no airport of its own. **Frankfurt Airport** is the usual gateway, about an hour away by direct train or by airport shuttle bus, and Stuttgart Airport is a similar distance.

The town sits on Germany's **ICE high-speed rail network**, so trains from Frankfurt, Stuttgart, and beyond are frequent and fast. The main station, **Heidelberg Hauptbahnhof**, is a short tram or bus ride west of the Old Town, not within walking distance of the castle.

## Where should I stay?

Stay in or near the **Altstadt**, the Old Town. From there the castle, the Old Bridge, and the long pedestrian Hauptstrasse are all on foot, and you get the quiet early-morning town the day-tripper never sees.

For a larger hotel near the river and a short walk from the Old Town, [Heidelberg Marriott Hotel](/pins/heidelberg-marriott-hotel) sits on the Neckar with views toward the castle. Beyond that the atlas does not yet carry a wide hotel pin set for Heidelberg, so book on the usual sites and read recent reviews for the property.

## The castle and the Old Town

**Heidelberg Castle** is the sight, a part-ruined Renaissance palace on the hillside, never fully rebuilt after French destruction and lightning damage, which is part of its character. Inside are the famous Great Tun, an enormous 18th-century wine barrel, and the German Pharmacy Museum. The [Heidelberg Bergbahn](/pins/heidelberg-rathaus-bergbahn), the funicular from the Kornmarkt, carries you up to the castle and on to the Königstuhl summit.

The **Altstadt** below is one of the longest intact old towns in Germany, threaded by the **Hauptstrasse**, a pedestrian street that runs nearly a mile end to end. Along it are the Marktplatz, the Church of the Holy Spirit, and **Heidelberg University**, founded in 1386 and the oldest in the country. The [Lindt Boutique](/pins/lindt-boutique-heidelberg) is one of the Hauptstrasse shops if a chocolate stop appeals.

The **Old Bridge**, formally the Karl Theodor Bridge, crosses the Neckar at the edge of the Old Town, with its twin-towered gate and the small brass monkey statue that visitors line up to touch.

## The Philosophers' Walk

The [Philosophers' Walk](/pins/philosophers-way), the Philosophenweg, climbs the Heiligenberg on the north bank of the river, opposite the castle. It is named for the university professors who once walked it, and it gives the postcard view: the castle, the Old Town, and the Old Bridge lined up across the Neckar.

The path is a steady uphill from the bridge, and late afternoon is the time to go, when the light falls on the castle face. Allow an hour or more for the round trip and the stops to look back.

## Where to eat and drink

Heidelberg eats on a mix of old brewhouses and a student-town range of cheaper kitchens.

[Vetter's Alt Heidelberger Brauhaus](/pins/vetter-s-alt-heidelberger-brauhaus) is the Old Town brewpub, known for house beer and German plates. [Südseite Coffee Roasters and Bakery](/pins/sudseite-coffee-roasters-and-bakery) covers coffee and baked goods for the morning. [Asia Heidelberg](/pins/asia-heidelberg-freunde-der-sichuan-kuche) cooks Sichuan food, and [Gastro ART5](/pins/gastro-art5-gmbh) is another of the city's pinned restaurants. None carry notes yet, so treat the list as a starting point rather than a ranking.
