[Antwerp](/cities/antwerp) is Belgium's second city, a port of about 530,000 people on the River Scheldt in Flanders. It carries a Rubens-era old city, the center of the global diamond trade, a serious avant-garde fashion reputation, and one of the most beautiful railway stations in the world. A day or two covers it. This guide covers getting in, where to stay, the old city, the fashion and diamond districts, and where to eat.

## How long do I need?

One day covers the old city, the Cathedral of Our Lady, and the riverfront. A second day adds the fashion district, the MAS museum out on the docks, and the Rubens House.

Antwerp pairs easily with Brussels, Ghent, and Bruges on a wider Belgium trip, all close by rail. As a stop on its own, a day or two is right.

## How do I get to Antwerp?

Antwerp sits on Belgium's fast rail network. Trains from **Brussels** take about 45 minutes, and **Brussels Airport** is roughly 35 minutes away by direct train. International services and the Eurostar connect through Brussels.

You arrive at **Antwerpen-Centraal**, and the station is a sight in itself, a monumental 1905 building with a vast domed waiting hall, often named the finest railway station in Europe. The old city is a short walk or metro ride away, and the center is walkable once you are there.

## Where should I stay?

Stay in or near the **old city**, the historic core around the Grote Markt and the cathedral, which puts the main sights on foot. The **station quarter**, lively and well connected, is the other convenient base.

## The old city and the cathedral

The **Cathedral of Our Lady** is the centerpiece, a soaring Gothic church with a single tall spire over the old city, and inside it hangs a set of major altarpieces by **Peter Paul Rubens**, the painter who made Antwerp an art capital. The **Grote Markt**, the main square, is ringed by guild houses and the Renaissance city hall, with the Brabo fountain at its center retelling the legend behind the city's name.

Down at the river, **Het Steen** is the small medieval castle on the Scheldt, the oldest surviving building in Antwerp. The **Rubens House**, the painter's own home and studio, sits a short walk away and is open as a museum.

## Fashion, diamonds, and the docks

Antwerp's modern reputation rests on two trades. The **diamond district**, a tight cluster of streets beside Central Station, handles a large share of the world's rough diamonds. The **fashion scene** dates to the 1980s "Antwerp Six" designers and continues through the city's fashion academy and the **MoMu** fashion museum in the design quarter.

North of the old city, the regenerated docklands hold the **MAS**, the Museum aan de Stroom, a tall red sandstone museum of the city and the port, with a free rooftop view over Antwerp.

## Where to eat

Antwerp eats well, on Flemish cooking, the famous Belgian fries and beer, and a broad casual scene across the old city and the docks.
