[Durres](/cities/durres) is the beach end of an Albania trip, the country's main seaside town, with one of the biggest Roman amphitheatres in the Balkans sitting right in the middle of it. It is 40 minutes from Tirana, so it is an easy add. Here's how I'd fit it in.

## How long do I need?

The historic sights take a few hours, so a half-day covers the city itself. If the beach is the point, Durres becomes a multi-day stay sized to how much sea time you want.

Many travelers see Durres as a day trip from Tirana, which works well for the Roman amphitheatre and the old core. As a beach base for a wider Albania trip, it can hold a longer stay.

## How do I get to Durres?

Durres sits about 40 minutes from **Tirana**, Albania's capital, by road, with frequent buses and *furgon* minibuses linking the two. **Tirana International Airport** is the nearest airport, roughly 30 to 40 minutes away.

Durres is also a working **ferry port**, with services across the Adriatic to Italy. The city itself is walkable along the shore and the old core, with the beach strip stretching south.

## Where should I stay?

For a beach stay, base along the **beach strip** south of the center, where most of the hotels sit close to the sand. For the historic sights and a quick Tirana connection, the **old core** near the port and the amphitheatre is the more central choice.

## The city and the beach

The standout sight is the **Durres Amphitheatre**, a Roman arena built into a hillside in the second century, one of the largest in the Balkans and sitting right in the modern town. Nearby, the round **Venetian Tower** survives from the medieval walls, now a bar with a view, and the **archaeology museum** along the seafront holds finds from the city's long history as a Greek and Roman port.

The **beach** is the other half of Durres, a long run of sand stretching south of the center, lined with hotels, cafes, and beach clubs. It is busy and lively through the summer and quiet off-season.

## Where to eat

Durres eats on Adriatic and Albanian cooking: grilled fish and seafood from the port, Italian-influenced pasta and pizza, and grilled meats.
