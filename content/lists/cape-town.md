Cape Town is the kind of place where a pin list is more useful than a neat top ten. The city spreads across the mountain, the city bowl, the Atlantic coast, the Waterfront, the peninsula, the Constantia side, the Winelands, and a few longer day trips. Distance looks manageable on a flat map, but the day changes once weather, traffic, and street-by-street safety enter the picture.

I would use this page as the working map behind my longer [Cape Town planning brief](/posts/cape-town-travel-brief). Start with where you are staying, then group the pins by geography: [Table Mountain](/pins/table-mountain-national-park) and outdoor time, the Waterfront and [Robben Island](/pins/robben-island), Sea Point and the coast, [Boulders](/pins/boulders-penguin-colony) and Cape Point, or the wine estates outside the city.

The point is not to finish every save. Cape Town works better when the plan has room. Pick one large outdoor plan, one food or market stop, and a transport plan that gets you home without turning a good evening into a judgment call.
