[Eastbourne](/cities/eastbourne) is a quiet, old-fashioned seaside town on the south coast, and the reason to come is right next door: Beachy Head and the Seven Sisters, the great white chalk cliffs of England. A day does the town, an overnight gets you the cliff walk. Here's how I'd split it.

## How long do I need?

A day covers the town: the seafront, the pier, and the Towner gallery. The Beachy Head and Seven Sisters cliff walk wants a second half-day, which is the argument for an overnight.

Eastbourne is a calm, traditional resort rather than a lively one, long popular with older visitors. The combination is what makes the trip: a tidy Victorian seafront beside dramatic chalk cliffs.

## How do I get to Eastbourne?

**Southern Railway** runs direct trains from **London Victoria** to Eastbourne in about an hour and a half. The [Eastbourne train station](/pins/eastbourne-train-station-southern-railway) sits in the town center, a short, flat walk from the seafront.

By car, Eastbourne is roughly two hours from London on the A22 and A27. Inside the town you do not need transport, and in summer an open-top bus runs the coast road toward Beachy Head.

## Where should I stay?

Stay on or near the **seafront**. The Victorian promenade has the sea views and the grand hotels, and the town center directly behind it has the station, the shops, and the restaurants, all within a short walk.

The landmark address is the [Grand Hotel](/pins/grand-hotel), the white Victorian "White Palace" at the western end of the seafront, the kind of formal old hotel the town was built around. Beyond it the atlas does not yet carry a wide hotel pin set for Eastbourne, so book on the usual sites and read recent reviews for the property.

## The seafront and the pier

The **seafront** is the heart of the town, a long landscaped promenade above the shingle beach, with the carpet-bed flower displays Eastbourne is known for and a Victorian bandstand that still runs a summer concert program.

**Eastbourne Pier** is the 1870s pleasure pier reaching out over the water, with its domed buildings, an amusement arcade, and tea rooms. Inland from the front, the [Towner](/pins/light-at-towner) is the town's contemporary art gallery, its building wrapped in a bold geometric mural, and the strongest cultural stop in Eastbourne.

## Beachy Head and the Seven Sisters

This is the reason to come. **Beachy Head** is the highest chalk sea cliff in Britain, rising more than 160 meters straight from the water about three miles west of town, topped by a small red-and-white lighthouse below. The clifftop path climbs straight out of Eastbourne, and the walk along the edge is a couple of hours of open downland and sea.

Continuing west, the **Seven Sisters** are the rolling run of chalk cliffs that follow, the view that stands in for the white cliffs of England in countless photographs. The full walk from Eastbourne over Beachy Head to the Seven Sisters and Birling Gap is one of the best day walks on the south coast. Stay back from the edge, which is unfenced and undercut.

## Where to eat

Eastbourne eats on seafront fish and chips, tea rooms, and a small spread of international kitchens in the town center.
