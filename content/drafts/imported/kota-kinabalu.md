Kota Kinabalu, the capital city of Malaysia's Sabah state nestled on the northern coast of Borneo, is a captivating blend of modernity and natural splendor. Framed by lush rainforests and the azure waters of the South China Sea, the city exudes a unique charm. Its bustling markets, vibrant street food scene, and a diverse cultural tapestry reflect the rich heritage of the region. Dominated by the majestic Mount Kinabalu in the distance, Kota Kinabalu offers not only a gateway to exploring Borneo's wilderness and wildlife but also an urban hub where traditional and contemporary lifestyles harmoniously coexist.

Tips for those who have never visited Malaysia 

#### 1. Malaysia is a predominantly Muslim country

In the capital, you may never notice anything different other than the call to prayer. In Kota Kinabalu however, it will be more noticeable. Your hotel breakfast will probably not have bacon or ham. Restaurants may be marked Halal or non-Halal. 

If you are a light sleeper, ensure that your hotel is not next to a mosque. I personally love hearing the adhan in the morning. Others I've travelled with felt it grew old after a day or so. 

#### 2. As a result, alcohol will not be as cheap as elsewhere in Southeast Asia

Expect to pay around $5-6 for a beer at a hotel or restaurant. Obviously this is less expensive than New York, London, or Singapore. In general the cost will be 2-3x what you will pay in Thailand. 

#### 3. Malaysian society is relatively conservative

Shops will cater to those tastes. Furthermore tanning is not as in vogue as in the western world. I had a hard time finding a tank top among the kiosks selling swimwear last time I needed to purchase a last minute shirt. However you will find tons of great SPF resistant long sleeve swimwear. 

As someone who burns easily, Kota Kinabalu was a great place to stock up on inexpensive surf skins. 

#### 4. If you plan to rent a car, be aware that you drive on the left side

Your car will be right hand drive. If you rent a vehicle with a manual you will shift with your left hand instead of the right. Fuel is incredibly cheap due to government subsidies. 

#### 5. There are no direct long-haul flights to Kota Kinabalu

This means that you will definitely connect somewhere like Kuala Lumpur or Singapore. Both are great places to spend a couple days. If you choose to extend your trip, be aware: 

* The Kuala Lumpur airport is 90ish minutes away from the city center without traffic. Prices are however low. You can expect to pay $75-125 for many nice 5* hotels. 

* Singapore is the opposite. It is easy to get to the center of town from Changi in just 15-20 minutes. However it is one of the most expensive countries in the world. 2 beers may set you back $30-40. 

### Why visit Borneo? 

Visiting Borneo offers a distinct and compelling experience compared to a destination like Thailand. Borneo's allure lies in its unparalleled biodiversity, pristine rainforests, and unique cultural encounters. Unlike the bustling tourist hubs of Thailand, Borneo's natural landscapes remain largely untouched, providing an opportunity to witness rare wildlife like orangutans, pygmy elephants, and proboscis monkeys in their natural habitats. 

The island's indigenous cultures offer an authentic and immersive experience, allowing travelers to connect with traditions that have been preserved for generations. Additionally, Borneo's ecotourism initiatives promote responsible travel and conservation efforts, making it a destination for those interested in sustainable and meaningful journeys. 

Ok, you're sold and found a cheap flight. Here's the stuff I would see

Borneo, a sprawling expanse of land, boasts numerous vibrant cities within its boundaries. To focus the scope of this blog, I will delve into topics that revolve around areas located within a one-hour radius of Kota Kinabalu.

Within a one-hour drive of Kota Kinabalu, you'll discover a wealth of natural wonders that showcase Borneo's breathtaking biodiversity. One such gem is the Kinabalu Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to Mount Kinabalu, Southeast Asia's highest peak. The park encompasses diverse ecosystems, from lowland rainforests to subalpine meadows, fostering an array of flora and fauna found nowhere else. Poring Hot Springs, situated nearby, offers rejuvenating thermal baths and the opportunity to explore canopy walkways through the lush jungle.

The Tunku Abdul Rahman Marine Park, a short boat ride away, boasts a collection of picturesque islands with pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs. Snorkeling and diving in these waters reveal a vibrant marine world, teeming with colorful fish and marine life.

For a glimpse into Borneo's endemic wildlife, the Lok Kawi Wildlife Park introduces you to various native species, including orangutans, proboscis monkeys, and sun bears. Nature enthusiasts can also venture to the Kawa Kawa River, known for its fireflies that create enchanting displays during nighttime boat tours. These are just a few examples of the natural treasures within an hour's reach of Kota Kinabalu, making it a paradise for those seeking outdoor adventures and ecological exploration.
