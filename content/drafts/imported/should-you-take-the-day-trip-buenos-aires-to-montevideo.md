## First, is Montevideo worth visiting?

![Montevideo rambla and beach.](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Montevideo/montevideo_rambla.jpg)
Is the picture of above appealing to you? If so, you might like Montevideo. 

The capital and largest city of Uruguay, Montevideo is situated on the southern coast of South America.

I know the city for it's rich culture of developers. 
*Shoutout ->
 I first came to Montevideo when I was working with a group of developers called [Lagarsoft](http://www.lagarsoft.com).

However, if you are coming from North America, Montevideo can be difficult to get to.

![mvd](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Montevideo/mvd.gif)

There are only directly flights from Miami. Everyone else will connect at least once in Brazil, Panama, Colombia, or Argentina. 

My suggestion: add a visit to Montevideo on to your trip to Argentina. Take the ferry directly from the center of Buenos Aires to Ciudad Vieja in Montevideo.

![The ferry from Montevideo to Buenos Aires is superior to flying. ](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Montevideo/IMG_1529.jpeg)

*The image above is the first class cabin, it's not much more expensive (20-30%) than a regular ticket. In exchange you get larger chairs and a much better view.

The Buquebus (ferry) boards [in Puerto Madero in Buenos Aires](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Terminal+Buquebus/@-34.5977944,-58.3716175,17z/data=!4m10!1m2!2m1!1sBuquebus!3m6!1s0x95a3353bfb4eb281:0xa6f85e987b43abe6!8m2!3d-34.5977946!4d-58.3679822!15sCghCdXF1ZWJ1cyIDiAEBkgENZmVycnlfc2VydmljZeABAA!16s%2Fg%2F11g18w74py?entry=ttu). You do have to go through customs and security, but it's nowhere near as invasive as an airport. You also pre-clear customs in Argentina. This means that the moment you walk off the boat, you can walk into town. Two and a half hours after you board, you'll be able to view Montevideo on the horizon.

![Approaching MVD from B/A](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Montevideo/IMG_1526.jpeg)

You will disembark in the [Port of Montevideo](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Port+of+Montevideo/@-34.9022412,-56.2086178,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x0:0x91938048737d51a5?sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjV39TQvaeAAxXorYkEHYjaB-4Q_BJ6BAhdEAA&ved=2ahUKEwjV39TQvaeAAxXorYkEHYjaB-4Q_BJ6BAhrEAg) which is adjacent to Ciudad Vieja. If you checked a bag, you'll need to grab that first. If not, head right out the door as you already cleared customs in Argentina.

The Mercado is a tourist trap, but it's a fun one. You can buy typical handicrafts and Uruguayan souvenirs. [Alfajores](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Alfajores+del+Uruguay.+Store./@-34.905551,-56.2320494,14z/data=!4m10!1m2!2m1!1salfajores!3m6!1s0x959f7f8450e169e3:0xdd48d28132230d00!8m2!3d-34.9052211!4d-56.211462!15sCglhbGZham9yZXNaCyIJYWxmYWpvcmVzkgEJY2FmZXRlcmlhmgEjQ2haRFNVaE5NRzluUzBWSlEwRm5TVVJOZUV0cVYxZFJFQUXgAQA!16s%2Fg%2F11b6j5c8xk?entry=ttu) are always a good present for colleagues, relatives, or neighbors. 

My suggestion: if you are hungry. Wander around the market as long as possible, then get a table at [Don Garcia.](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Don+Garc%C3%ADa/@-34.9055528,-56.2118361,3a,75y,90t/data=!3m8!1e2!3m6!1sAF1QipMs6m-lLH4TDLNkT-pDlyh3CRuSH9Da4ZBWStiw!2e10!3e12!6shttps:%2F%2Flh5.googleusercontent.com%2Fp%2FAF1QipMs6m-lLH4TDLNkT-pDlyh3CRuSH9Da4ZBWStiw%3Dw203-h154-k-no!7i4314!8i3279!4m7!3m6!1s0x959f7f845826e595:0x58c03eddc47d59a5!8m2!3d-34.9055528!4d-56.2118361!10e5!16s%2Fg%2F1pp2vmzz7?entry=ttu) 

![Mercado del Puerto Asado](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Montevideo/DSC_8740.jpeg)
Be prepared to sit for at least 2 hours. Food is ordered ala carte, but portions are large enough to share and will come out as they are ready. 

Your asado lunch & subsequent walk around the old town & beach will take up the better part of a day. If you're a day tripper you will likely need to head back to the port at this point. 

If you're staying a bit longer

I usually head straight to my hotel and drop my stuff off from the ferry. You can find taxis directly outside the terminal. For the most part, they are honest, it will help if you know a few words of Spanish. 

While Uber is legal in Uruguay and works*, there is an asterisk. It is very difficult to get an Uber and most times you will wait at least 10 minutes for a pickup.

If you stopped for lunch, you can find taxis [towards the port here](https://www.google.com/maps/@-34.9049885,-56.2110881,3a,75y,226.41h,84.8t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1stZiBuu68qi1Xv92jQx3hDg!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu). Uber does work in Montevideo, but there are not many drivers and you will need to wait. On average it takes 15-20 minutes to get a car. 

If you did not take the ferry, I'm truthfully very sorry. Here is what you will need to know:

You'll land at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) which is 40 minutes outside of the city. I've only successfully ordered an Uber from the airport 1 time.

You can take one of the airport taxis into the city. They are clean, safe, and generally very nice (I often see MB E-Class wagons). You will however generally pay a pretty hefty price, approximately 50-60$ last time I took one.

![IMG_1557](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Montevideo/IMG_1557.jpeg)If you are a hotel traveler, you will not find many typical Western chains in Montevideo. ## What to do in Montevideo

The capital of Uruguay is blessed with scenic coastline and pristine beaches along the Rìo de la Plata. The beaches in Montevideo are pretty easy to find. They run the length of the city with the exception of the port, marinas, etc. 

[Playa Pocitos](https://www.google.com/search?q=playa+pocitos+montevideo&rlz=1C5CHFA_enES1060ES1060&tbm=lcl&sxsrf=AB5stBjvIN8xGnWiXsTMlAoXtMA_zjoBzA%3A1690206642265&ei=soG-ZKzRD-KkptQP1-20sAQ&ved=0ahUKEwis8oi0vqeAAxVikokEHdc2DUYQ4dUDCAk&uact=5&oq=playa+pocitos+montevideo&gs_lp=Eg1nd3Mtd2l6LWxvY2FsIhhwbGF5YSBwb2NpdG9zIG1vbnRldmlkZW8yBRAAGIAEMgYQABgHGB4yBhAAGAcYHjIEEAAYHjIGEAAYBRgeMggQABiKBRiGAzIIEAAYigUYhgNItQZQ4gFY-wNwAHgAkAEAmAG4AaABzwSqAQMwLjS4AQPIAQD4AQHCAgQQIxgniAYB&sclient=gws-wiz-local#rlfi=hd:;si:;mv:[[-34.9096501,-56.1398417],[-34.919435299999996,-56.148811099999996]]) is the most famous and typically most crowded. [Playa Ramirez](https://www.google.com/search?q=playa+ramirez+montevideo&rlz=1C5CHFA_enES1060ES1060&tbm=lcl&sxsrf=AB5stBhjx_WYYD_5jW3JZRp1cwuBiUz3Pw%3A1690206645750&ei=tYG-ZPS1LbKE0PEP5pSOmAg&oq=playa+ramires+montevideo&gs_lp=Eg1nd3Mtd2l6LWxvY2FsIhhwbGF5YSByYW1pcmVzIG1vbnRldmlkZW8qAggAMgcQABgNGIAEMgcQABgNGIAEMggQABgIGB4YDTIIEAAYigUYhgMyCBAAGIoFGIYDMggQABiKBRiGAzIIEAAYigUYhgNIhg9QjwFYrQdwAHgAkAEAmAFZoAHABKoBATe4AQHIAQD4AQHCAgQQIxgnwgIEEAAYHsICBhAAGAgYHsICBhAAGAUYHsICCBAhGKABGMMEwgIFEAAYogSIBgE&sclient=gws-wiz-local) is just as nice, slightly less crowded, and more relaxed. 

Be aware, the river is often brown so don't expect a Carribean style white sand beach. Most of the city is residential right up to the coast. You also will not find tons of waterfront style restaurants or bars.

![DSC_8573](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Montevideo/DSC_8573.jpeg)
However if you explore long enough, you will find one of my favorite places to chill. One of [El Parador](https://www.google.com/maps/place/El+Parador/@-34.9154718,-56.1487894,19.43z/data=!4m10!1m3!2m2!1sRestaurants!6e5!3m5!1s0x959f815d5aed96d9:0x37af4940b408e4ca!8m2!3d-34.9153845!4d-56.1493164!16s%2Fg%2F11rp9jgpdx), a surf shack on the Rambla on Playa de Pocitos, if you go there, tell Cristian (cool dude pictured above) that "Mike sent you". You can also just say: 

*"Este gringo Mike me mandó a tu bar. Dijo que es el mejor lugar para pasar tiempo en Montevideo."*

At that point, kiss goodbye to any other plans you had for the evening and enjoy your Cerveza Zillertal. Cheers 🍻!
