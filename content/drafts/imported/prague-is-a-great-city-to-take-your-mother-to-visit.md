You may remember Prague as a backpacker's heaven with cheap alcohol and a hip scene that lasted way into the evening. There are still opportunities to find all of the above, but you are more likely to pay $4-5 USD for a small hot wine in the old town square. Charles Bridge had more people than Times Square during my last visit.

### | 
Why should I take my mother to Prague?

I'll start with some assumptions. If your mother is like mine, she loves to travel. The wish list for a 10/10 European Vacation might roughly look like this: 

* 
Cool medieval architecture with old castles or impressive historic buildings

* 
A walkable old town with cool shops and funky things to eat

* 
Enjoying meals or drinks from a corner cafe on bustling streets

* 
Good vantage points to view the historic old town

* 
A museum or attraction that has something you can't see elsewhere

 She also likes to set and keep to a budget. You can get all of this in Prague.

## Tips for first timers planning a trip to Prague:

Americans have limited vacations. If you're planning to fit another major city in to your itinerary I would suggest Munich, Vienna, or Berlin. You can reach of them by direct train in ±4 hours. That is similar to a door to door time for a 1-2 hour flight as you will travel from the center of one city to another and do not need to go through security.
