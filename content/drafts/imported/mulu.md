# Mulu

## Gunung Mulu National Park in Sarawwak Malaysia is the coolest national park you never knew existed. 

|
 
Be prepared for a trek to get there.

![DSC_2964](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/DSC_2964.png)

## Why Visit Gunung Mulu National Park?

Traditional print media marketing got me. Why wouldn't I want to visit a distant rainforest in Borneo with a Marriott resort attached? 

![IMG_4842](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/IMG_4842.png)If you want better reasons I'll give you 3:

* 
Gunung Mulu National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It's renowned for its flora and fauna, especially the bat colonies.

* 
The park features one of the largest cave systems in the world. The entrances to Deer Cave are over 140m tall.

* 
The cave systems have left stunning Karsk formations including the Pinnacles

#### ![Mulu Airport](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/IMG_4964.png)| Flight routing is difficult.

Full stop. Gunung mulu national park is located in a remote portion of Borneo near the Brunei border. You can only reach Gunung Mulu by Air (unless you are very adventurous and take a boat). Mulu Airport (MLU) is small by any standards.

Each day there are 4 flights operated on ATR72s by MasWings (owned by Malaysian Airlines). Often the flight is less than 20% full. You may have your seats moved around to balance the load.

Working backwards, your final leg will go through one of the following cities:

* Miri

* Kuching

* Kota Kinabalu

### I would route through Kuching or Kota Kinabalu. Take a few days and check out the parks there. 

Buying round trip tickets into Gunung Mulu will not save you any money over buying roundtrip flights. You can theoretically stop at one airport on your way in, and another on the way out. To pick your layover I would ask myself: 

Would I prefer to island hop and see some amazing beaches, or check out a national park that has [proboscus monkeys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proboscis_monkey)?

![IMG_4960](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/IMG_4960.jpeg)You could [stay in the park lodge](https://mulupark.com/accommodations-facilities/), however there is no wifi. For reference, beds in the hostel run about $11/night while villas run about $65+.

![IMG_4815](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/IMG_4815.jpeg)There are less than 10 homestays and hotels in the whole area. By far the best option is the [Mulu Marriott Resort & Spa.](https://www.marriott.com/en-us/hotels/myymu-mulu-marriott-resort-and-spa/overview/) It typically runs about $100/ night. Marriott status members will be pleased to note that occupancy tends to be low. Your chance at a large suite upgrade is high so save your suite night awards. 

The Marriott has a pair of on-demand shuttles that will take you to park headquarters. You don't even need to worry about booking them, they'll be at the airport when the flights arrive.

![IMG_4960](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/IMG_4960.jpeg)The hotel is built on stilts and large portions are open-air. Reception, the bar, and pool areas are the only portions of the property that have wifi (even the suites). I did however have 2 bars of 4G service in my room which was enough for a hotspot.

## Speaking of 🦇... the main reason to visit 

![DSC_2964](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/DSC_2964.png)There are typically two types of visitors to Mulu. The first is there to see caves and bats. 

For this category of tourists (myself included) there are approximately 10km of "unguided hiking trails" (ie boardwalk) split into[ 5 separate walks](https://mulupark.com/tours-activities/unguided-tour/). Aside from the stairs in the Canopy Tour, none of these will require any strenuous exertion or need to pack beyond water and bug spray. 

![IMG_4913](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/IMG_4913.png)The park also offers guided group walks that go into some of the protected areas. These guided walks are the only way to see some of the best attractions such as Deer, Lang, and Clearwater Caves. The Cave tours all have a fair number of stairs, especially the Clearwater Cave (see left, yes that is a staircase). 

All of the tours are inexpensive (<10$/person each). They are somewhat limited in spots, so you should try to book these before you go. You'll need to [manually contact the park staff](https://mulupark.com/contact-us/), they keep bookings in a physical book.

#### The other type of visitor has more time, energy, and risk acceptance than me.

The most famous spot in the park is the Pinnacles. It requires an overnight 3-day trek to visit. Thus my use of the photo from Wikipedia below.

![mulu Stock image - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Mulu_Pinnacles.jpg](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/Mulu/mulu.jpg)

[- photo attribution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Mulu_Pinnacles.jpg)

The climb is relatively strenuous and has checkpoints you must meet in order to reach the summit. Once you are there, you have a set window to see the formation before you need to return to camp.
