## Before you board the airplane read this:

* 
**Houston is a car city. Do not depend on public transit.**

Houston sprawls. It's hot. There are no sidewalks. There is no subway or rapid transit. In 99% of cases you will need a car to get around. If you are staying for more than a day a rental car is your best bet. For in/out trips of a day or less I usually take Uber (or bum a ride from a friend)

* 
Houston is not "Southern" but is still incre 

asdf

* **Don't plan on a beautiful beach vacation on the coas**

![bcn tram](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/BCN/bcn%20tram.jpg) You can pay with cash or card in the machines in the subway stations. Trams will have a ticket machine at the stop or on board. The best way to find your way around is literally Google Maps. Select transit options to choose only subway, tram, bus, etc.

* **If you are combining your trip with a visit to Madrid, take the train.
**The train station (Sants) is in the center of the city so you will save at minimum 30 euros on each end by avoiding the taxi to/from the airport. Today there are 3 operators, IRYO, OUIGO, and Renfe. Check all three for the best option or price. 
Tickets are dynamically priced so are more expensive the closer you book. It's possible to get a 1 way ticket for about €10 from IRYO or OUIGO if you book well enough in advance. 

* **Save some money by picking a hotel away from Plaza Catalunya or Barrio Gótico**
Barcelona has always been a popular destination, but it's packed post-COVID. Expect to pay over €200/ night for an average hotel in these areas. Hostel prices are high as well.

#### Sants 

For those coming for just a weekend on the train, staying at Sants may be a good choice to save time. Like many neighborhoods in Europe with a major train station, there may be transients around. This is a convenience choice. 

👍 Pros: If you arrive on the train, you're already at your hotel. Convenient location to Plaza Catalunya and the other tourist attractions, they are close, not next door. 

👎 Cons: Transient population, not aspirational.

🏢 My Hotel Rec: [AC Hotel Sants](https://www.google.com/maps/place/AC+Hotel+by+Marriott+Sants/@41.3774366,2.1360697,17z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m9!3m8!1s0x12a498869467710f:0xc51ea81a94fb6beb!5m2!4m1!1i2!8m2!3d41.3774326!4d2.1386446!16s%2Fg%2F1tdqqpjt?entry=ttu)

#### Enjoy spectacular city panoramic views from Tibadabo 
![IMG_9724](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/BCN/IMG_9724.jpeg)

Tibidabo is a mountain in Barcelona, Catalonia, known for its amusement park and stunning views of the city. Founded in 1901, it is one of the oldest amusement parks in the world. The park offers a range of attractions, including thrilling rides, a vintage carousel, a Ferris wheel, and a beautiful church, the Temple Expiatori del Sagrat Cor, at its peak. Tibidabo is open on weekends and during holidays throughout the year, as well as during the summer season.

## My favorite paid activities in Barcelona

A couple of these are a bit removed from the city center. I'll leave it up to you to determine what your priorities are for your holiday. I mention them because these are things I would 100% do again but may take a bit of hustle to reach.

#### Explore a real life Labyrinth 

![IMG_6731](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/BCN/IMG_6731.jpeg)The [Laberint d'Horta](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Parc+del+Laberint+d'Horta/@41.4399738,2.1429964,17z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m6!3m5!1s0x12a4bd5fbcfc78d5:0xddcd3164482e2203!8m2!3d41.4399698!4d2.1455713!16s%2Fm%2F05f62w4?entry=ttu) is a hedge maze located within the Parc del Laberint d'Horta in Barcelona's Horta-Guinardó district. 

Take the Metro L3 (Green Line) to Mundet station, followed by a 10-15 minute walk to the park entrance. The entrance fee is just over €2 per person. 

Navigate the labyrinthine paths created by tall hedges in search of the center, where a statue of Eros, the Greek god of love, awaits. During the colder months, the Labyrinth may be a bit bare. 

![Barcelona Velodrome](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/BCN/Screenshot%202023-05-30%20at%2011.40.01%20AM-1.png)Just outside is the [municipal velodrome](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Velodrome+of+Horta/@41.4405752,2.14273,16z/data=!4m6!3m5!1s0x12a4bd5ea40ee04f:0x1b6c195b38971d8a!8m2!3d41.4381017!4d2.148016!16zL20vMGduc2tw?entry=ttu) which has a bar. For a couple euros you can catch the cyclists and have a refreshment.

#### Visit 
Park Güell

![IMG_6698 (1)](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/BCN/IMG_6698%20(1).jpeg)The park was built between 1900 and 1914 is my favorite work by Antoni Gaudí. It was was originally intended to be a residential complex inspired by the English garden city movement. However, the project was not successful, and only two houses were constructed. 

Of all the Gaudi works in Barcelona, this one is my personal favorite. It's relatively inexpensive: tickets are 10€ per person (compared with €30ish for other attractions) I'd expect to spend at least 1-2 hours here. 

Be aware there is some climbing involved so it's not an activity for people who want to walk around. It's located towards the northwest of the city so a good activity to combine with seeing Casa Vicens and some of the other famous houses. 

#### 
Visit Sagrada Familia

![IMG_3190](https://25168707.fs1.hubspotusercontent-eu1.net/hubfs/25168707/Travel/BCN/IMG_3190.jpeg)No blog is complete without mentioning Barcelona's most famous tourist attraction. You're already going to visit here, so here are some tips to make it easy for you: 

* You'll have to go through security to enter. Don't bring knives, or drugs. You also cannot bring outside food or drink in, so dump it before they scan your bag. 

* Tickets are 
€26 for the base price. There are no steep discounts. For example students and those under 30 receive €2 off tickets. Entering the tower costs extra. 

* 
It gets crowded and sells out. Book your tickets ahead of time for peace of mind and go early to avoid the crowds.
